Inversion

Decision Making
5 Min Read • Charlie Munger • Core Model

"Approaching a situation from the opposite end of the natural starting point."

The concept of inversion is rooted in the mathematical principle developed by Carl Jacobi, who famously advised, "man muss immer umkehren" (invert, always invert). It is a cognitive tool that forces you to view a problem from the reverse angle. Instead of focusing on the desired outcome and the steps to get there, you focus on the worst possible outcome and the steps that would lead to it.

Charlie Munger popularized this framework for modern decision-making, particularly in investing and life strategies. He recognized that it is often easier to identify and avoid stupidity than it is to seek brilliance. In complex systems, solving forward is prone to miscalculation, whereas solving backward reveals glaring pitfalls. By systematically avoiding the actions that guarantee failure, you inherently increase the probability of success.
