Concept A Mental Model

The Inversion Principle

"Avoid stupidity is easier than seeking brilliance."

Definition

A framework that requires looking at situations from the opposite end to uncover hidden assumptions and avoid catastrophic errors.

Methodology

Subtractive View:

Instead of "How do I win?", ask "How do I ensure I don't lose?"

Concept B Cognitive Psychology

Confirmation Bias

"What the human being is best at doing is interpreting all new information so that their prior conclusions remain intact."

Definition

The tendency to search for, interpret, favor, and recall information in a way that confirms or supports one's prior beliefs or values.

Mechanism

Additive Error:

The brain actively collects "proof" for existing models while filtering out contradictory data.

Synthesis Insight

The Antidote Loop

Inversion acts as a structural circuit-breaker for Confirmation Bias. By forcing the mind to actively seek failure modes, it creates a "forced disconfirmation" that bypasses the brain's natural tendency to only seek supporting evidence.
