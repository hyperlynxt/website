Epistemology

Map is not the Territory

The abstract representation of a thing is not the thing itself. Models fall short of reality because they must reduce complexity to be useful. Recognizing the boundaries where a map fails is as critical as knowing how to read it.

Strategy

Inversion

Instead of asking how to achieve a desired outcome, ask how to guarantee the opposite. By aggressively identifying and avoiding paths of failure, success often becomes the only remaining trajectory.

Systems

Second-Order Effects

Every action has a consequence, and every consequence has a consequence. Failing to consider the chain reaction of decisions leads to solving one problem while generating a more complex, structural failure elsewhere. The initial solution may prove catastrophic over longer temporal horizons.

Logic

Occam’s Razor

When presented with competing hypotheses that make the same predictions, one should select the solution with the fewest assumptions. Simplicity implies a lower probability of structural error.

Behavioral

Hanlon’s Razor

Never attribute to malice that which is adequately explained by stupidity. A crucial framework for preserving cognitive bandwidth and avoiding destructive paranoia in complex social ecosystems. It shifts the perspective from intent to error.

Risk Analysis

Margin of Safety

Building systems capable of handling loads significantly heavier than anticipated. It acknowledges the inherent limits of human foresight and the inevitability of structural stress.

Cognitive

First Principles

Boiling a process down to the fundamental, undeniable truths and reasoning up from there, rather than reasoning by analogy. The core mechanism for architectural disruption. It requires stripping away existing biases and assumptions to see the raw materials of a problem.
