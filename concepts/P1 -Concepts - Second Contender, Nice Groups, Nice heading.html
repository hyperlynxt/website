Featured Model

Map is Not the Territory

The representation of a thing is not the thing itself. Our perception of the world is a model, flawed and reduced by our own processing limits.

Cognitive

Confirmation Bias The tendency to search for, interpret, favor, and recall information in a way that confirms preexisting beliefs. First Principles Thinking Breaking down a complicated problem into its most basic, foundational truths that cannot be deduced further. Dunning-Kruger Effect A cognitive bias in which people with low ability at a task overestimate their ability.

Systems

Feedback Loops A system structure where outputs are routed back as inputs as part of a chain of cause-and-effect. Tragedy of the Commons Shared-resource systems where individual users acting independently behave contrary to the common good. Pareto Principle The observation that for many events, roughly 80% of the effects come from 20% of the causes.

Philosophy

Occam's Razor The problem-solving principle that "entities should not be multiplied beyond necessity." Veil of Ignorance A method of determining morality requiring decision-makers to know nothing of their own identity. Hanlon's Razor Never attribute to malice that which is adequately explained by stupidity or carelessness.
