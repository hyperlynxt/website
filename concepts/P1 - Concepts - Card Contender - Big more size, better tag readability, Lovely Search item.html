01
Decision Making

Inversion

Approaching a situation from the opposite end of the natural starting point. Instead of asking how to achieve success, ask how to guarantee failure, and avoid those actions.
02
Systems Thinking

Map is not the Territory

The description of the thing is not the thing itself. Models are reductions of reality designed to be useful, not perfectly accurate. Confusing the model with reality leads to critical errors in judgment when edge cases arise.
03
Physics

First Principles

Boiling a complex problem down to its most fundamental, indisputable truths, and reasoning up from there, rather than reasoning by analogy or prior conventions.
04
Cognitive Bias

Confirmation Bias

The tendency to search for, interpret, favor, and recall information in a way that confirms or supports one's prior beliefs or values.
05
Economics

Opportunity Cost

The value of the next best alternative forgone as the result of making a decision. Every choice implies a rejection of another path.
06
Systems Thinking

Second-Order Effects

Every action has a consequence, and each consequence has another consequence. Failing to consider the secondary or tertiary effects of a change often leads to worse outcomes than the original problem.
07
Math

Compounding

The process where the value of an investment increases because the earnings on an investment, both capital gains and interest, earn interest as time passes. Applies strictly to knowledge and habits.
08
Decision Making

Occam's Razor

When presented with competing hypotheses about the same prediction, one should select the solution with the fewest assumptions. Simpler explanations are generally better.
