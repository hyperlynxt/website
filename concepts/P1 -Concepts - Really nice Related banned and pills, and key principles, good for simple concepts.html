Mental Model

The Inversion Principle

"Avoid stupidity is easier than seeking brilliance. Instead of asking how to achieve a goal, ask how you could guarantee failure—and then don't do that."

Core Definition

Inversion is a problem-solving framework that requires looking at a situation from the opposite end. Rooted in mathematics, it forces you to uncover hidden assumptions and avoid catastrophic errors.

Most people approach complex systems additively. Inversion forces a subtractive view: "What behavior guarantees failure?"

Forward Thinking

Focuses on steps required for success. Often blindsided by unseen risks.

Inverted Thinking

Focuses on identifying causes of failure. Structurally sound and risk-averse.

Application

  • 01Define the clear goal.
  • 02Invert the goal to imagine failure.
  • 03Identify the causes of that failure.
  • 04Build systems to avoid those causes.
